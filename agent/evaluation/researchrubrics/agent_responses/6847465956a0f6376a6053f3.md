Modern Text-to-Speech (TTS) models up to 2025 have made significant advancements in prosody, emotion, and multilingual capabilities, with emerging tasks like voice cloning, zero-shot synthesis, and cross-lingual voice transfer gaining traction. Here's a comprehensive evaluation:

1. **Prosody Control**: Modern TTS systems, such as those from ElevenLabs and NVIDIA Riva, provide fine-grained control over pronunciation and prosody through markup languages like Speech Synthesis Markup Language (SSML) <cite id="3bb120ff-0">. These models can generate speech with natural intonation and rhythm, enabling more expressive and human-like output. The integration of multi-scale emotion modeling in architectures like the one described in the arXiv paper further enhances prosody by preserving emotional nuance and speaker-specific characteristics <cite id="3bb120ff-3">.

2. **Emotion Synthesis**: Emotion-controllable TTS models, such as the Index-TTS framework, allow for the generation of speech with specific emotional tones through multiple input modalities <cite id="3bb120ff-2">. These models can produce a wide range of emotions, from neutral to excited or sad, ensuring that the synthesized speech aligns with the intended emotional context. The ability to maintain appropriate emotional tone across 30+ languages is also a key feature of modern systems, as highlighted by the Smallest AI blog <cite id="3bb120ff-4">.

3. **Multilingual Capabilities**: TTS models have expanded to support a wide range of languages, with systems like CAMB.AI's MARS8 family and NVIDIA Riva offering multilingual support that preserves regional accents and transliteration <cite id="3bb120ff-5">. The paper on multilingual TTS with accents and emotion modeling demonstrates advancements in handling diverse linguistic and cultural contexts <cite id="3bb120ff-3">. Additionally, zero-shot prosody transfer enables models to adapt to new languages without explicit training data, enhancing their versatility <cite id="3bb120ff-9">.

4. **Emerging Tasks**:
   - **Voice Cloning**: NVIDIA Riva TTS models and other advanced systems have demonstrated the ability to clone voices with high fidelity, capturing speaker-specific characteristics and emotional nuances <cite id="3bb120ff-5">.
   - **Zero-Shot Synthesis**: Models like the one described in the arXiv paper achieve zero-shot prosody transfer, allowing them to generate speech in new languages or with new accents without additional training <cite id="3bb120ff-3">.
   - **Cross-Lingual Voice Transfer**: Modern TTS systems can transfer voice characteristics across languages, maintaining emotional expression and prosody while adapting to new linguistic contexts <cite id="3bb120ff-9">.

In summary, modern TTS models have evolved to produce highly expressive, emotionally rich, and multilingual speech, with emerging capabilities in voice cloning, zero-shot synthesis, and cross-lingual voice transfer. These advancements are driven by innovations in deep learning architectures and the integration of multi-modal input for enhanced control and expressiveness.
