A realistic timeline leading to an Axis victory in World War II would require a series of critical events and strategic decisions that diverge from historical outcomes. According to alternate history scenarios, <cite id="824be74d-2">Nazi Germany must decisively win the war in Europe and conquer all of Russia as Lebensraum</cite>, while Japan successfully conquers the Pacific and Southeast Asia. This would involve Germany achieving a quick victory over France in 1940, <cite id="824be74d-1">executing Generalplan Ost to establish Lebensraum in Eastern Europe</cite>, and Japan securing dominance in the Pacific through a successful attack on the United States. 

If the Axis powers had won, the world would have been dominated by Nazi Germany and Imperial Japan, <cite id="824be74d-5">emerging as the two new superpowers</cite>. The post-war world would likely have seen the establishment of a totalitarian global order under Axis control, with significant changes in geopolitics, technology, and culture. <cite id="824be74d-7">Triumph of the Will centers around a global conflict between Imperial Japan and Nazi Germany in 1947, after a World War II where the Axis powers utterly crush the Allies</cite>. This would have led to a world where Axis ideology shaped global governance, and the development of technology and science would have been heavily influenced by Axis priorities.

The present day would be vastly different, with a world order dominated by Axis powers, <cite id="824be74d-1">more and more indoctrinated, ideological, and generally more oppressive</cite>. The absence of the United States and the Soviet Union as major powers would have altered the Cold War dynamics, and the global spread of Axis ideology would have had profound effects on human rights, scientific progress, and international relations.
