The impact of automation on employment and society is a complex issue with multiple potential scenarios. According to research, automation can both create and destroy jobs, with the net effect depending on factors such as technological advancement, economic structure, and policy responses. For instance, a study published in 2023 found that industrial robots increase total employment in developed countries, but this effect is not found in developing countries (Filippi, 2023). This suggests that the impact of automation may vary significantly across different economic contexts.

In the first scenario, where automation leads to mass unemployment and there are no universal resources provided to everyone, the societal structure would likely experience significant challenges. Without universal resources, the population increase could exacerbate inequality and social unrest. A report from McKinsey & Company (2017) suggests that while automation may lead to job losses in certain sectors, it could also create new jobs in others. However, the transition period could be difficult, with some workers unable to adapt to the new job market. The Brookings Institution (2022) notes that automation often creates as many jobs as it destroys over time, but this depends on the ability of workers to acquire new skills.

In the second scenario, where automation leads to mass unemployment but universal resources are available to everyone, the societal structure would be different. With universal resources, the need for traditional employment might diminish, leading to a potential shift in the economic and social systems. The MIT Economics Department (2023) explores the history and future of work, suggesting that automation could lead to a redefinition of work and the role of the individual in society. In this scenario, the focus might shift from employment to other forms of contribution and personal fulfillment.

The impact of automation on employment and society is multifaceted, with both challenges and opportunities. The specific outcomes would depend on a variety of factors, including technological development, economic policies, and social structures. As automation continues to advance, it is crucial to consider the potential implications and develop strategies to mitigate negative effects while harnessing the benefits of technological progress.
