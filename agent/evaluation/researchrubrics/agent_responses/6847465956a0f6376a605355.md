Artificial intelligence (AI) is transforming the drug discovery process, which traditionally takes 3–6 years and costs billions of dollars. AI-driven tools can streamline this process by predicting drug efficacy, toxicity, and optimal dosages, as noted in <cite id="60286137-0">a 2024 study</cite>. Recent advancements in AI have enabled its application across every stage of drug development, from target identification to clinical trials, as highlighted in <cite id="60286137-1">a 2025 review</cite>.

At the target identification stage, AI algorithms analyze vast biological datasets to identify potential therapeutic targets. For example, AI can predict protein structures and interactions, which is critical for understanding disease mechanisms. <cite id="60286137-3">A Harvard Wyss Institute report</cite> emphasizes that AI's ability to detect patterns in complex data makes it ideal for this phase.

In the lead optimization phase, AI accelerates the design of drug candidates by predicting molecular properties and interactions. <cite id="60286137-5">AbbVie's 2024 case study</cite> shows how AI is used to optimize drug design and improve therapeutic outcomes. Additionally, <cite id="60286137-6">a 2022 review</cite> highlights applications such as virtual screening, de novo drug design, and retrosynthesis prediction.

Recent technological advancements include the integration of generative AI models like GANs and transformers, which enable the creation of novel molecular structures. <cite id="60286137-8">Isomorphic Labs</cite> demonstrates how AI can model biological phenomena to design new molecules. Furthermore, <cite id="60286137-4">a 2025 study</cite> notes that AI is revolutionizing traditional models by combining data, computational power, and algorithms.

Despite these advancements, challenges remain. <cite id="60286137-9">A 2023 review</cite> points out issues such as data quality, model interpretability, and regulatory hurdles. The FDA has recognized the growing use of AI in drug development and is working to establish guidelines for its adoption, as mentioned in <cite id="60286137-2">an FDA 2026 report</cite>.

Currently, AI is being widely adopted in the pharmaceutical industry. <cite id="60286137-7">A 2023 article</cite> states that AI tools are reshaping the speed and economics of drug discovery. Companies like AbbVie are already leveraging AI to accelerate discovery and improve drug design. Overall, AI is playing a pivotal role in modernizing drug discovery, though challenges must be addressed for broader implementation.
