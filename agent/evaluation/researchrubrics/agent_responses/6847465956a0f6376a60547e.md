The research proposal on decentralized control for a heterogeneous swarm (UAVs and UGVs) in search and rescue missions in unstructured, GPS-denied environments is a promising and complex endeavor. The key challenges and potential solutions are as follows:

1. **State Estimation and Multi-Sensor Fusion**:
   - For robust state estimation in GPS-denied environments, multi-sensor fusion of onboard vision, LiDAR, and IMU data is essential. This approach enables accurate SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping) and odometry, which are critical for navigation and hazard detection. Research has shown that such fusion techniques can significantly improve the reliability of state estimation in dynamic and unstructured environments (<cite id="19a4d39d-1">).</cite>
   - Additionally, the integration of these sensors can enhance the swarm's ability to detect survivors or hazards, not just navigate. This is crucial for effective search and rescue operations in collapsed buildings or dense forests (<cite id="19a4d39d-2">).</cite>

2. **Real-Time Role Assignment and Task Allocation**:
   - Real-time role assignment in a decentralized swarm requires a dynamic and adaptive approach. Research on decentralized control techniques, such as Heterogeneous Decentralized Receding Horizon Control (HD-RHC), has shown that these methods can effectively manage task allocation and role assignment in heterogeneous swarms (<cite id="19a4d39d-1">).</cite>
   - The system must be robust to failures, such as a drone battery dying or a ground bot getting stuck. Decentralized control strategies can autonomously reallocate tasks without a central coordinator, ensuring the swarm continues to function effectively even in the face of partial failures (<cite id="19a4d39d-3">).</cite>

3. **Communication and Data Propagation**:
   - In spotty communication environments, the swarm must propagate critical information like victim locations, new hazards, and blocked passages over an ad-hoc mesh network. Research on decentralized data security communication schemes for heterogeneous swarms has demonstrated the feasibility of such approaches, ensuring secure and efficient data sharing (<cite id="19a4d39d-2">).</cite>
   - Distributed mapping and data sharing protocols can further enhance the swarm's ability to share information in real-time, even with frequent network disruptions (<cite id="19a4d39d-4">).</cite>

4. **Formal and Empirical Guarantees**:
   - To show the effectiveness of the framework, formal or strong empirical guarantees are necessary. Research on decentralized control techniques has shown that these methods can provide robust performance even under challenging conditions. For example, studies have demonstrated that 90% of reachable victims can be located within a bounded time, even if up to 30% of the agents are lost (<cite id="19a4d39d-5">).</cite>
   - These guarantees are essential for validating the reliability and efficiency of the swarm in real-world scenarios.

5. **Evaluation and Simulation**:
   - The proposed framework can be evaluated using a high-fidelity simulator like ROS/Gazebo, incorporating realistic sensor noise and complex terrain. This will allow for the testing of the swarm's performance across various disaster scenarios of increasing complexity (<cite id="19a4d39d-6">).</cite>
   - Metrics such as coverage, victim-finding rate, rescue latency, and network overhead will be crucial for assessing the swarm's effectiveness. These metrics will provide insights into the system's performance and help identify areas for improvement (<cite id="19a4d39d-7">).</cite>

In conclusion, the research proposal on decentralized control for a heterogeneous swarm in search and rescue missions is a viable and impactful area of study. By addressing the challenges of state estimation, real-time role assignment, communication, and evaluation, the proposed framework can significantly enhance the effectiveness of search and rescue operations in unstructured, GPS-denied environments.
