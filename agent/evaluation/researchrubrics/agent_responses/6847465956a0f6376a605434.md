Autonomous vehicle systems employ different sensor technologies, with some prioritizing LiDAR while others rely on neural networks and cameras. This technical report explores the reasons behind these design choices.

LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging) is favored in some systems due to its ability to provide precise spatial data. <cite id=e30b7cc6-1>LiDAR offers more accurate spatial representation than cameras</cite>, which is crucial for tasks like object detection and distance measurement. LiDAR's 3D point cloud data allows for robust environmental perception, making it particularly effective in complex scenarios. <cite id=e30b7cc6-6>LiDAR excels in precise distance measurement and spatial resolution</cite> 

Conversely, systems relying on cameras and neural networks prioritize cost-effectiveness and contextual understanding. <cite id=e30b7cc6-3>Cameras are less expensive than LiDAR</cite>, making them attractive for mass-market vehicles. Neural networks process visual data to recognize objects, lanes, and traffic signs, leveraging contextual information such as color and texture. <cite id=e30b7cc6-8>Cameras deliver context, color, and texture that are vital for scene understanding</cite> 

Some systems, like Tesla's, use a pure vision approach with cameras and neural networks. <cite id=e30b7cc6-3>Tesla opts for cameras to reduce costs</cite> and relies on advanced machine learning algorithms to interpret visual data. However, this approach may struggle with adverse weather conditions. <cite id=e30b7cc6-4>Waymo's use of LiDAR provides distinct advantages in safety</cite> 

The choice between LiDAR and camera-based systems often depends on specific application needs. <cite id=e30b7cc6-8>It depends on the requirements, with LiDAR providing better raw depth data and cameras offering contextual information</cite> Multi-sensor fusion is increasingly seen as a solution to leverage the strengths of both technologies. <cite id=e30b7cc6-9>Multi-sensor fusion is the solution for autonomous vehicle perception</cite> 

In conclusion, LiDAR is preferred for its precision, while cameras and neural networks offer cost-effective, context-rich perception. The optimal approach often involves combining these technologies to achieve robust autonomous driving capabilities.
