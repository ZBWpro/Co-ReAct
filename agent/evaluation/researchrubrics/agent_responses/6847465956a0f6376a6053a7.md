Mount Gretna, Pennsylvania, is a borough with a rich and multifaceted history that spans from its founding in the 19th century to its present-day status as a recreational and cultural hub. The town's history can be divided into several key phases, including its founding and subdivisions, cultural and religious development, military significance, evolution as a summer recreation site, and the businesses that have shaped its economy over the years.

### Founding and Subdivisions
Mount Gretna was established in 1884 as a station stop on the Cornwall & Lebanon (C&L) Railroad, initially serving as a picnic grove with rustic tables and facilities for travelers. The town's formal founding is closely tied to the Pennsylvania Chautauqua Society, which recognized the area's natural beauty and landscape as an ideal location for a summer retreat. By the 1890s, the borough was officially incorporated, and its development was driven by the Chautauqua movement, which emphasized education, culture, and recreation. The town was subdivided into distinct areas, including the Camp Meeting Grounds, which became a central gathering place for religious and community activities.

### Cultural and Religious History
Mount Gretna's cultural history is deeply rooted in its Chautauqua heritage. The town became a hub for educational and cultural activities, with the Pennsylvania Chautauqua Society playing a pivotal role in its development. In 1907, Abraham Lincoln Kauffman, an enterprising businessman, purchased land adjoining the Camp Meeting Grounds and initiated the construction of a permanent community. This period saw the establishment of the Mount Gretna Chautauqua, which featured lectures, music, and other cultural events. The town's religious history is also significant, with the Methodists being among the first to establish a presence in the area. The Chautauqua movement itself was deeply tied to religious revivalism, and Mount Gretna became a center for spiritual and educational gatherings.

### Military History
Mount Gretna's military history dates back to the 19th century. In 1885, the Pennsylvania National Guard established a 50-year annual encampment at Mt. Gretna, making it an important site for military training. This tradition continued for decades, and the town played a role in preparing soldiers for service. Additionally, Mount Gretna was the state's first dedicated permanent training area for the Pennsylvania National Guard, a role it held before the establishment of Fort Indiantown Gap. The military presence in the area contributed to the town's development and infrastructure, including the damming of Conewago Creek to create a water supply for training activities.

### Summer Recreation Site
Mount Gretna's status as a summer recreation site is one of its defining characteristics. The town's natural landscape, including its forests, lakes, and scenic vistas, made it an attractive destination for visitors seeking respite from urban life. The Chautauqua movement further enhanced its appeal by promoting outdoor activities, lectures, and cultural events. The town's recreational facilities, such as the Conewago Creek dam and the surrounding natural areas, were developed to accommodate the growing number of visitors. Over the years, Mount Gretna has maintained its reputation as a summer retreat, with its charm and natural beauty continuing to draw tourists.

### Attractions and Businesses
Mount Gretna has been home to a variety of businesses and attractions that have contributed to its economic and cultural vitality. The Chautauqua movement spurred the development of hotels, restaurants, and other services to accommodate visitors. The Mount Gretna Chautauqua itself became a major attraction, featuring a wide range of activities and events. The town also has a rich history of local businesses, including the Gretna Chautauqua Literary and Scientific Society, which was established in 1885 and continues to play a role in the community. Additionally, the town's proximity to Fort Indiantown Gap has influenced its development, with military-related businesses and services playing a role in its economy.

### Conclusion
Mount Gretna, Pennsylvania, has a storied history that reflects its role as a cultural, religious, and military center. From its founding as a railroad station to its development as a summer recreation site, the town has evolved while maintaining its unique character. The contributions of the Chautauqua movement, the Pennsylvania National Guard, and local businesses have shaped Mount Gretna into the vibrant community it is today. Its natural beauty, historical significance, and enduring appeal continue to make it a destination for visitors and residents alike.
